Set up a standard 4-stage Amber MD simulation for a small protein in explicit water.

System details:
- Protein PDB file: protein.pdb (located in the working directory)
- Force field: ff19SB for the protein
- Water model: OPC with a 10 Angstrom solvation buffer
- Add Na+ and Cl- counterions to neutralize and reach 150 mM ionic strength

Simulation stages:
1. Energy minimization: 2000 steps (1000 steepest descent, then conjugate gradient)
2. NVT heating: heat from 0 K to 300 K over 50 ps (25000 steps, dt=0.002 ps)
   - Use a harmonic restraint of 10 kcal/mol/A^2 on all heavy atoms during heating
3. NPT equilibration: 100 ps at 300 K and 1 bar
   - Remove restraints, use Monte Carlo barostat
4. NPT production: 10 ns (5,000,000 steps, dt=0.002 ps) at 300 K and 1 bar

Settings for all MD stages:
- SHAKE on all bonds involving hydrogen (ntc=2, ntf=2)
- 10 Angstrom non-bonded cutoff
- Langevin thermostat (gamma_ln=1.0)
- Random seed (ig=-1)
- Write coordinates every 10 ps, print energy every 1 ps

Write:
- tleap.in (system preparation script)
- mdin_min.in (minimization)
- mdin_heat.in (NVT heating with restraints)
- mdin_equil.in (NPT equilibration)
- mdin_prod.in (NPT production)
