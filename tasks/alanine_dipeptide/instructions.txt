Set up a 3-stage Amber MD simulation for alanine dipeptide in explicit water.
This reproduces Amber Tutorial B0 (Simple Simulation of Alanine Dipeptide).

System preparation (tleap.in):
- Build alanine dipeptide as the ACE-ALA-NME sequence (no PDB file needed)
- Force field: ff19SB (source leaprc.protein.ff19SB)
- Water model: OPC (source leaprc.water.opc)
- Solvate with an octahedral box, 10 Angstrom buffer (solvateoct, OPCBOX)
- Save topology and coordinate files as parm7 and rst7

Simulation stages:

1. Energy minimization (01_Min.in):
   - 2000 total steps: first 1000 steepest descent, then conjugate gradient
   - Non-bonded cutoff: 8.0 Angstrom
   - No trajectory written (ntwx=0)
   - Print every 100 steps (ntpr=100)

2. NVT heating (02_Heat.in):
   - 10000 steps, dt=0.002 ps (= 20 ps total)
   - Heat from 0 K to 300 K using NMROPT temperature ramps:
       steps 0-9000: 0 K → 300 K
       steps 9001-10000: hold at 300 K
   - Constant volume (ntb=1, ntp=0)
   - SHAKE on all bonds involving hydrogen (ntc=2, ntf=2)
   - Langevin thermostat (ntt=3), collision frequency gamma_ln=2.0
   - Non-bonded cutoff: 8.0 Angstrom
   - Random seed (ig=-1)
   - Write trajectory every 100 steps (ntwx=100), print every 100 steps (ntpr=100)

3. NPT production (03_Prod.in):
   - 5,000,000 steps, dt=0.002 ps (= 10 ns total) at 300 K
   - Read coordinates AND velocities from restart file (ntx=5, irest=1)
   - Constant pressure (ntb=2, ntp=1) with Berendsen barostat (barostat=1)
   - SHAKE on all bonds involving hydrogen (ntc=2, ntf=2)
   - Langevin thermostat (ntt=3), collision frequency gamma_ln=2.0
   - Non-bonded cutoff: 8.0 Angstrom
   - Random seed (ig=-1)
   - Write trajectory every 100 steps (ntwx=100), print every 100 steps (ntpr=100)

Write exactly these four files:
- tleap.in
- 01_Min.in
- 02_Heat.in
- 03_Prod.in
